If the soldier mentioned above, registered in this office, after the call-up to actual military service in the regular army, brings with him his own boots and underwear, suitable for use, then at his arrival to the army [quarters], those things may be turned in to the State Treasurer and he will be reimbursed: for a pair of boots with the tops not shorter than 15 inches – 5 rubles; for a shirt – 50 kopecks; for underwear – 35 kopecks; these items are to be submitted in quantities not greater than one pair of boots, two shirts and two pair of underwear.

Notes:


1. The original said that Height was 2 yards [or] 42 vershoks and the boots in the last paragraph had to have tops no shorter than  9 vershoks. The vershok is an old Russian measure about 4.4 cm or 1 3/4 inches.


2. The word “Petit-bourgeois” is used in place of the Russian word мещанйи (meshchanin) which means “member of urban lower-middle class”


3. Shmul (or Shmuel) is Samuel in Yiddish. Iankel (or Yankel) is Jacob and Leibov (son of Leib) lion.


4. The seal says “District Military Service Office of the City of Pruzhany”


5. This translation was done by Dr. Dorota Rzymska of Slavic Language Services.

EXCERPT FROM THE STATUTES REGARDING

COMPULSORY MILITARY SERVICE


        1)  Each year after the end of admitting people to military service in the regular army, administrative district offices, district offices or city offices enroll all other persons on the basis of a lot list as [potential] conscripts to fulfill compulsory military service with the exception of those who, from a military point of view, are not qualified to carry weapons (art. 51).  The maximum age for persons recruited as conscripts is 40 years (art.

        2)  National conscription is called only in the extraordinary circumstances of war (art. 5).

        3)  National conscription is discontinued at the end of war, or sooner, when it is no longer needed (art. 40).

        4)  For those [persons] who are included as conscripts a certificate is issued with a lot number which serves as verification of their fulfillment of compulsory military service (art. 160).

        5)  The Certificate Regarding Compulsory Military Service is to be presented when a marriage is contracted and also when one is hired by a state agency (art. 100 and art. 163).
