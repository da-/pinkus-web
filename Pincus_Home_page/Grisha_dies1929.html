Rosalie recalls the death of Uncle Grisha (Hirsch).


 
 
 

There appears to be a bit of a mystery concerning the given name of the uncle referred to in this clip. His crossed out name and photo from the family’s 1917 German exit permit appear at the right where it clearly says Hirsch. On the other hand, Rosalie always called him Grisha and recalls that her brother George was named after him. On the third hand, Grisha is also a diminuative for the Yiddish names Hirsh, Hersh, Khersh, Gersh, and Girsh. On the fourth hand, according to one expert: Pronunciation of "H" is not clear in Russian. Sometimes it is sound as "H" (russian "Х") though it is often substituted for the sound/letter "G" (russian "Г"). So, after all this research was the name of the man in the picture Hirsch or Grisha? The answer, without any doubt, is YES.
